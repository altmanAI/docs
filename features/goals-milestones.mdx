A goal without a path is a wish. DailyPilot's Goals & Milestones feature bridges the gap between the life you want and the day in front of you by breaking long-term ambitions into concrete milestones and surfacing the right daily actions at exactly the right time. Pilot AI does the heavy lifting of decomposition so you can stay focused on doing the work, not managing the system.

## Creating a Goal in DailyPilot

Navigate to the **Goals** section from your main dashboard. Every goal in DailyPilot is built around three things: a clear name, a target date, and the reason it matters to you. That third element — the *why* — is what Pilot AI uses to make its suggestions relevant rather than generic.

Goals can be personal, professional, health-related, creative, or anything else that genuinely matters to you. DailyPilot does not impose a category structure; your goals are organised by your own language and values.

## How Pilot AI Decomposes Your Goals

Once you create a goal, Pilot AI analyses the name, target date, and personal context you've provided and suggests a set of milestones — meaningful checkpoints that mark real progress on the way to the finish line. For each milestone, Pilot AI suggests the daily or weekly actions most likely to get you there.

This is not a generic project template. Pilot AI tailors its suggestions to the specific goal and timeframe you've described, and it adjusts as you provide feedback. If a suggested milestone doesn't fit your reality, you edit or remove it. The goal is always yours; Pilot AI is the thinking partner, not the planner.

## Live Progress Tracking

Every milestone and daily action you mark complete is reflected in real time on your goal's progress view. You can see at a glance:

- **Overall goal progress** — a visual indicator of how far you've come relative to your target date.
- **Milestone status** — which checkpoints are complete, in progress, or not yet started.
- **Daily action history** — a log of which actions you've completed and when, drawn directly from your [Daily Check-Ins](/features/daily-check-ins).

The connection between goals and check-ins is intentional. When you open your morning check-in, DailyPilot surfaces the daily actions tied to your active milestones. You're never left wondering what to work on — the system connects intent to execution automatically.

## Setting Up Your First Goal

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Goals">
    From your DailyPilot dashboard, select **Goals** in the main navigation. You'll see your goal board — empty at first, and growing over time.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create a new goal">
    Click **New Goal** and fill in three fields: a clear, specific goal name; a realistic target date; and a sentence or two about why this goal matters to you personally. The *why* field is not optional — it's the anchor that keeps you aligned when motivation fluctuates.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Let Pilot AI suggest milestones">
    After you save the goal, Pilot AI will generate a suggested milestone roadmap. Review the suggestions — they appear as a proposed timeline with recommended checkpoints and estimated action cadence.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review and adjust the milestones">
    Edit any milestone that doesn't match your reality. Change dates, rename checkpoints, remove suggestions that don't apply, or add milestones Pilot AI didn't think of. Your goal, your roadmap.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Start your first daily action">
    Activate one of the daily actions attached to your first milestone. The next time you open your morning check-in, that action will appear in your priority suggestions — connecting your long-term goal directly to today's work.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  When you write your goal's *why*, go beyond the surface. "Get fit" is a goal. "Build the physical capacity to hike with my kids without stopping" is an anchor. The more specifically your goal connects to your personal values and identity, the more reliably Pilot AI's suggestions — and your own motivation — will stay aligned with what truly matters.
</Tip>
