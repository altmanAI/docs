Intentions are easy to remember fondly. Actions are harder to distort when they're written down. The Impact Ledger is DailyPilot's permanent, auditable record of what you actually did — not a curated highlight reel, but a factual account of your daily actions, completed milestones, and real-world progress over time. It exists because the gap between who we think we are and who we are actually becoming is often invisible until someone shows us the evidence.

## What the Impact Ledger Is

The Impact Ledger is a transparent, chronological log built from your real activity inside DailyPilot. Every meaningful action you take — completing a check-in, marking a milestone achieved, logging a reflection, making progress on a goal — is recorded automatically. You don't have to maintain it; it maintains itself.

Think of it less as a to-do list and more as a flight recorder: an objective account of what happened, when, and in what context. That record is yours, it is private by default, and it is built to be trusted.

## What Gets Logged

The Impact Ledger captures the following categories of activity:

- **Completed check-ins** — Each morning and evening check-in you finish, including the priorities you set, the commitments you made, and the mood you recorded.
- **Daily action completions** — Every goal-connected daily action you mark done, with a timestamp and the goal it belongs to.
- **Milestone achievements** — Milestones you reach, including the date completed and the goal they advanced.
- **Goal progress updates** — Incremental progress recorded against active goals over time.
- **Reflections** — Structured reflections from your daily, weekly, and milestone-triggered sessions, including what you learned and what you'd do differently.

## How to Read Your Impact Ledger

Access the Impact Ledger from your main dashboard. The default view is a **timeline** — entries appear in reverse chronological order so your most recent activity is always visible first.

Use the built-in filters to narrow the view:

- **By goal** — See all logged activity associated with a specific goal.
- **By entry type** — Filter to just check-ins, reflections, milestones, or goal actions.
- **By date range** — Pull up a specific week, month, or custom period.
- **Progress over time** — Switch to the progress chart to visualise your activity density and milestone pace across any date range.

The Impact Ledger is designed to be read, not just accumulated. Set aside a few minutes at the end of each month to scan your ledger. The patterns that emerge — consistent days, recurring blockers, the goals you keep advancing and the ones you keep deferring — are among the most honest feedback you will ever receive.

## Why Transparency Matters

Most personal productivity systems optimise for making you feel good about your intentions. DailyPilot is built to show you the truth about your actions.

The Impact Ledger distinguishes your **real trajectory** — what the evidence actually shows — from your **hoped-for trajectory** — the story you'd tell at a dinner party. Over weeks and months, those two lines sometimes align. When they diverge, the ledger shows you exactly where they parted ways and gives you the information you need to close the gap.

This transparency is not about judgement. It is about agency. You cannot change what you cannot see.

## The Impact Ledger and the P.A.I.H.I. Framework

The Impact Ledger is the practical expression of the **Proof** pillar inside DailyPilot's [P.A.I.H.I. Operating System](/paihi/proof). Proof is the principle that every insight, every assessment of progress, and every piece of feedback offered by Pilot AI must be grounded in real evidence — not hype, not assumption, not flattery.

When Pilot AI tells you that you're making strong progress, it is reading from your Impact Ledger. When it surfaces a pattern you haven't noticed, it found it in the ledger. The record is the foundation that makes everything else trustworthy.
