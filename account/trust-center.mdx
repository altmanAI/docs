DailyPilot is an official product of AltmanAI, hosted on the company's own domain at [altmanai.tech](https://www.altmanai.tech). This page explains the decisions behind how DailyPilot is built and distributed, what you can expect as the product evolves, and the standards AltmanAI holds itself to in its development process. Transparency isn't a footnote here — it's part of how the product is designed.

## Domain integrity

DailyPilot is hosted directly on **AltmanAI.tech**, the official company domain for AltmanAI. That choice is intentional, and it matters for a few reasons:

- **Direct company connection** — when you visit altmanai.tech, you are on AltmanAI's own infrastructure. There is no third-party distribution layer between you and the company behind the product.
- **No marketplace dependency** — hosting as a web app avoids reliance on native app marketplace policies during early-stage development. AltmanAI controls its own release cycle.
- **Authentic product identity** — the domain serves as AltmanAI's public brand home. A product hosted here has been intentionally placed there by the team building it.

If you ever want to verify you're using the real DailyPilot, check that the URL is `altmanai.tech`. That's the only official address.

## What you should know as a user

AltmanAI is committed to being straightforward about what DailyPilot is today and where it's headed:

- **DailyPilot is a product of AltmanAI**, hosted on the official AltmanAI.tech domain. It is not a third-party app or an unofficial build.
- **The platform will change.** Features are actively being developed, tested, and improved. Layouts, options, and capabilities may look different over time — that's a sign the product is being worked on, not abandoned.
- **Web distribution is a deliberate choice** — it prioritises speed of delivery, broad accessibility, and the ability to respond quickly to user feedback.
- **An App Store release may come later.** As DailyPilot matures, native app distribution will be evaluated. There's no firm timeline, but the option remains open.
