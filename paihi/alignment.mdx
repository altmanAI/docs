It's easy to be busy. It's harder to be busy at the right things. The **Alignment pillar** in DailyPilot addresses the most common gap in personal productivity — the space between what you say matters most and what you actually spend your time on each day. Alignment doesn't judge that gap; it makes it visible so you can do something about it.

## What Alignment means in DailyPilot

Alignment is the continuous connection between your daily actions and the values and goals you've named as important. Most productivity tools track what you do — Alignment asks *why* you're doing it and whether that reason still connects to the life you're building.

The question Alignment keeps asking is: **Does what you're doing today serve what you said matters?** When the answer drifts, Pilot AI surfaces it. When the answer is clear, your check-ins reflect that clarity back to you as a source of momentum.

## The alignment loop

Alignment isn't a one-time setup — it's a cycle that compounds over time. Here's how it works in DailyPilot:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Set goals anchored to your values">
    Define what matters and why. Your goals connect to the values you named at onboarding, giving each one a clear reason for existing.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Break goals into milestones">
    With AI-assisted decomposition, each goal is broken into trackable milestones — concrete markers of real progress rather than vague ambitions.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Connect daily actions to goals">
    Each check-in links your daily priorities to specific goals. This is where alignment is either reinforced or where drift begins.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Reflect at the end of the day">
    Evening reflections close the loop — you assess what happened, what moved, and what you'd do differently. These reflections feed directly into Pilot AI's understanding of your patterns.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Adjust goals as you grow">
    Alignment isn't about rigidly sticking to what you decided six months ago. The loop surfaces when your goals may need to evolve — and gives you the data to make that call with clarity.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## How to use Alignment insights

When Pilot AI surfaces an alignment pattern, it will show you specifically which goals your recent actions have and haven't been serving. Here's how to get the most from those insights:

- **Don't dismiss drift as a bad week.** If Pilot AI surfaces the same gap across two or three weeks, that's a signal worth examining. Either your actions need to shift, or your goal is no longer reflecting what actually matters to you.
- **Use the mismatch as a decision prompt.** A misalignment between your stated priority and your actual behavior isn't a failure — it's information. Ask Pilot AI to help you think through whether the goal still fits, or where the friction is coming from.
- **Reconnect priorities to goals explicitly.** If your check-ins have been light on goal connections, take a few minutes in your next morning check-in to deliberately link each priority. That single step recalibrates the signal Pilot AI is reading.

<Tip>
  Every 90 days, revisit your values list. The goals you're chasing today are built on who you were when you wrote them. As you grow, your values evolve — and goals that once fit perfectly can quietly start pulling in the wrong direction. A 90-day values review is one of the most high-leverage habits you can build inside DailyPilot.
</Tip>

## How Alignment connects to the rest of P.A.I.H.I.

Alignment doesn't work in isolation. It draws from **Proof** — the data in your check-ins and Impact Ledger has to be real for alignment signals to be trustworthy. It's enforced through **Integrity** — Pilot AI surfaces misalignment even when it's uncomfortable to hear. It respects **Humanity** — you decide what to do with every insight, always. And it's validated by **Impact** — alignment is meaningful only when it produces measurable movement toward what you care about.
